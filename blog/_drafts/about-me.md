Hi! I&apos;m <span itemprop="name">Dennis</span>.
====

<p class="lead">
  I&apos;m currently a <span itemprop="jobTitle">senior developer</span> at YoungCapital. 
  Our team focuses on the job boards <a href="https://www.uitzendbureau.nl" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Uitzendbureau.nl</a> &amp; <a href="https://www.jobbird.com/nl/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Jobbird.com</a>.
</p>

During my study communication &amp; multimedia design at the Hogeschool van Arnhem en Nijmegen, I developed a real interest in creating complex software solutions with a focus on the user experience. For my graduation assignment, I was able to combine both at Uitzendbureau.nl: building a PHP application that combined the data from several systems through REST APIs, and allowing users to manage the data in those systems themselves via an easy to use jQuery and AJAX powered interface inside the Uitzendbureau.nl website itself.

After graduation I stayed at Uitzendbureau.nl to continue doing work on the front-end. Continuously balancing between the best experience for the users and an easy to develop and maintain application for our mainly back-end focused development team, we moved to a more structured foundation. In that foundation we used Sass for its consistency through easier reuse, Twitter Bootstrap for a well-documented, customizable component library that the whole team could work with and an improved JavaScript setup containing just jQuery for interactivity and AJAX requests.

In the meantime I had been doing chores in many parts of the back-end, which had increased my interest in it. Many of these chores were cumbersome or repetitive and I saw another opportunity to improve. I came up with one plan to incrementally refactor the procedural controllers of the custom MVC framework to an object-oriented implementation based on the ideas of big frameworks like Symfony and Zend Framework. Then I came up with a second plan to refactor the persistency layer of the application, from a custom ActiveRecord implementation to a DataMapper implementation using Doctrine.

These days as a senior developer I'm also mentoring our junior colleagues and guiding interns. Under my guidance we've now built several microservices, launched the same product under an additional brand and abroad, and have migrated to Vue. Besides that, I'm still searching and listening for issues the team is running into that we can improve on.

In my spare time I'm a big sports enthousiast, both in watching and doing. I do my best to have some fun in cooking. And I love being outside and enjoying nature.
